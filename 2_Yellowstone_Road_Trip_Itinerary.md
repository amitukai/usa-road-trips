# Hamilton, ON to Yellowstone National Park Road Trip Itinerary
**Trip Duration:** June 27 - July 11, 2026 (15 Days, 14 Nights)

## Trip Overview

### Route Summary
- **Outbound Route:** Hamilton → Michigan → Wisconsin → South Dakota → Wyoming → Yellowstone
- **Return Route:** Yellowstone → South Dakota → Iowa → Illinois → **Chicago (overnight)** → Michigan → Ontario
- **Total Distance:** ~5,700 km round trip
- **National Parks Pass:** America the Beautiful Annual Pass - $80 USD (~$110 CAD) - **Valid for 1 vehicle and all passengers**

### Key Highlights
- Badlands & Wind Cave National Parks
- Mount Rushmore & Crazy Horse Memorial
- Devils Tower National Monument
- Grand Teton National Park
- Yellowstone National Park (3 nights)
- **Chicago downtown overnight (Day 13)**
- Multiple scenic drives with minimal trekking
- Cedar Rapids overnight option (flexible)

## Important Notes & Tips

### National Parks Pass
- **America the Beautiful Annual Pass: $80 USD (~$110 CAD)**
- **Covers 1 vehicle and all passengers** - perfect for couples!
- Valid at: Yellowstone, Grand Teton, Badlands, Wind Cave, Devils Tower, Mount Rushmore, Indiana Dunes
- Purchase at first park (Badlands on Day 5) or buy online beforehand at [store.usgs.gov/recreationpass](https://store.usgs.gov/recreationpass)
- Physical pass ships within 3 weeks - allow time or buy at park
- Keep pass in glove box for easy access at park entrances

### EV Charging Strategy for Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV
- **PHEV charging planned at:** Mackinaw City, Rapid City, Cody, West Yellowstone (3 nights), Sioux Falls, Des Moines, Chicago, Kalamazoo
- All Level 2 overnight charging at hotels (220V, 3-5 hour full charge)
- **Cost:** Most hotel chargers are complimentary or $5-10 per session
- Can run hybrid mode continuously if needed - don't worry about charging gaps
- **ChargePoint and PlugShare apps** - find public charging stations en route
- Electric range: ~35-40 km per charge; use for city driving to save gas
- **Tip:** Request room near charging station when checking in

### Vehicle Preparation & Road Trip Essentials
- **Pre-departure vehicle inspection:**
  - Oil change if due within trip dates
  - Check tire pressure and tread depth (including spare)
  - Top off all fluids (coolant, brake fluid, windshield washer)
  - Test lights, wipers, horn
  - Check battery health

### Food Storage & Wildlife Safety - CRITICAL
**Wildlife Distance Rules:**
- **25 meters (75 feet):** Bison, elk, deer, moose, bighorn sheep
- **100 meters (300 feet):** Bears, wolves, coyotes
- **Use zoom lens** for photos - NOT zoom feet!

**Wildlife Behavior:**
- **Bison:** Unpredictable, can run 50 km/h, cause most injuries in Yellowstone
- **Bears:** Active dawn/dusk, attracted to food smells
- **Elk:** Aggressive during calving (May-June) and rut (Sept-Oct)
- **Moose:** Most dangerous - give widest berth

**Food Storage in Parks:**
- Never leave food in open vehicle
- Store in hard-sided containers or trunk
- No food in tent (if camping)
- Use bear-proof trash cans
- Don't eat in car then leave windows down

**If Wildlife Approaches:**
- Stay calm, don't run
- Back away slowly
- Make yourself big if bear approaches
- Get in vehicle if available
- Use bear spray only as last resort (within 7-10m)

**Wildlife Jams:**
- Pull fully off road (not half on shoulder)
- Stay in vehicle
- Don't contribute to crowding animals
- Be patient - jams can last 30-60 minutes

### Driving Safety - Mountain & Rural Roads
- **Speed limits:** Strictly enforced in national parks (45 mph / 70 km/h max)
- **Curves:** Many mountain roads have sharp hairpin turns (10-20 mph / 15-30 km/h)
- **Elevation:** Engine may feel sluggish above 2,500m - downshift as needed
- **Brakes:** Use low gear on descents to avoid brake fade
- **Wildlife crossings:** Dawn/dusk most dangerous - scan roadsides constantly
- **Fuel planning:** In Wyoming, gas stations can be 100+ km apart - fill at half tank
- **Fatigue:** Stop every 2 hours, rotate drivers
- **Weather changes:** Can go from sun to snow in 30 minutes at elevation

### Health & Safety Considerations
**Altitude Sickness Prevention:**
- Yellowstone is 2,300m (7,500 ft) - some may feel effects
- Symptoms: Headache, nausea, fatigue, dizziness
- **Prevention:** Hydrate (3-4L water/day), avoid alcohol first 24h, rest frequently
- **Treatment:** Descend to lower elevation, ibuprofen, hydrate

**Sun Protection:**
- UV rays 25-30% stronger at high elevation
- Reapply sunscreen every 2 hours
- Wear hat and sunglasses always
- Cloudy days still cause sunburn

**Hydration:**
- Bring 2L water per person per day
- Refill at visitor centers (free, filtered)
- Coffee/soda don't count - need plain water
- Signs of dehydration: Dark urine, headache, dizziness

**Medical Facilities:**
- **Yellowstone:** Clinics at Mammoth, Old Faithful, Lake (May-Sept only)
- **Emergency:** Dial 911 from any park phone
- **Nearest hospitals:** Jackson (WY), Cody (WY), Bozeman (MT)
- **Travel insurance:** Verify USA coverage for medical emergencies
