# Hamilton, ON to Yellowstone National Park Road Trip Itinerary

**Trip Duration:** July 4 – July 18, 2026 (15 Days, 14 Nights)  
**Travelers:** 2 Adults (Couple)  
**Vehicle:** Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV  

---

## Trip Overview

### Route Summary

- **Outbound Route:** Hamilton → Michigan → Wisconsin → South Dakota → Wyoming → Yellowstone
- **Return Route:** Yellowstone → South Dakota → Iowa → Illinois → Chicago (overnight) → Michigan → Ontario
- **Total Distance:** ~5,700 km round trip
- **National Parks Pass:** America the Beautiful Annual Pass — $80 USD (~$110 CAD) — Valid for 1 vehicle and all passengers

### Key Highlights

- Badlands & Wind Cave National Parks
- Mount Rushmore & Crazy Horse Memorial
- Devils Tower National Monument
- Grand Teton National Park (full dedicated day — Day 9)
- Lamar Valley wildlife corridor — wolves, bears, bison (Day 6 en route)
- Yellowstone National Park (3 nights)
- Chicago downtown overnight (Day 13)
- Multiple scenic drives with minimal trekking
- Cedar Rapids overnight option (flexible)

### Day 6: Thursday, July 9, 2026

**Route:** Cody, WY → Lamar Valley → Yellowstone Lake Overlook → West Yellowstone, MT  
**Distance:** 343 km | **Driving Time:** ~5h 10min (per Google Maps, via WY-296 & US-212 W)

> **Route Note:** This is the scenic Beartooth Highway approach entering Yellowstone through the **Northeast Entrance** via Cooke City, MT — considered one of the most spectacular drives in North America. You'll pass through Lamar Valley mid-route then exit via the park's south end past Yellowstone Lake before arriving at West Yellowstone. Note: this route has tolls (Northeast Entrance park fee — covered by your America the Beautiful Pass). Fill up gas in Cody before departing as there are limited stations on this mountain route.

#### Attractions & Stops

**Morning:**

- **Buffalo Bill Center of the West**, Cody (2 hours max — keep it brief to allow time for the drive) — Entry: $21 USD/person (~$58 CAD for two)
- Depart Cody by 10:00 AM; head north on WY-296 (Chief Joseph Scenic Byway)

**Mid-Morning:**

- **Chief Joseph Scenic Byway / WY-296** — dramatic canyon and mountain scenery; roadside pullouts along the way
- Enter Yellowstone via **Northeast Entrance** (near Cooke City, MT)

**Late Morning – Early Afternoon:**

- 🦬 **Lamar Valley** — "Serengeti of North America" (1.5–2 hours)
  - Best wildlife viewing in all of Yellowstone: bison herds, wolves, bears, elk, pronghorn
  - View entirely from car and roadside pullouts — no hiking needed
  - Drive the full Lamar Valley stretch slowly (~30 km); stop at pullouts as wildlife appears
  - **This is the best time of day for wildlife — late morning light is ideal**

**Afternoon:**

- Continue south through the park (Tower Junction → Canyon → Fishing Bridge)
- **Yellowstone Lake Overlook Trail area** — pullouts along the north shore of Yellowstone Lake
  - Lake Butte Overlook: stunning panoramic view over the lake (drive-up)
  - Fishing Bridge area: pelicans, osprey, cutthroat trout visible from bridge (walk across bridge — ~200 m)

**Evening:**

- Exit via West Entrance; arrive West Yellowstone (~5:30–6:00 PM)
- Walk around town, visit shops

#### 🚶 Walking Distances — Day 6

| Attraction | Parking Location | Walk to Viewpoint | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Buffalo Bill Center of the West | On-site lot | ~50 m to entrance | All indoor — keep visit to 2 hrs max |
| Chief Joseph Scenic Byway pullouts | Roadside pullouts | ~20–50 m from car | Mountain canyon views; drive-up |

| West Yellowstone town stroll | Hotel parking | ~100–200 m along Madison Ave | Flat, relaxed evening walk |

**🟢 Optimized Tip for Day 6:** Lamar Valley is 100% a car-window and roadside experience — you don't need to leave the vehicle at all unless you spot wildlife at a pullout. Budget 90 minutes to drive slowly through the full valley, stopping wherever herds appear. This maximises sightings with zero walking effort.

### Day 9: Sunday, July 12, 2026

**GRAND TETON — Full Day**  
**Route:** West Yellowstone → Grand Teton National Park → Jackson, WY  
**Distance:** ~180 km | **Driving Time:** ~2.5 hours driving + full day in park

> **Day 9 Focus:** With Lamar Valley now done on Day 6, today is entirely devoted to **Grand Teton National Park** — giving you a leisurely full day to enjoy the most photogenic mountain scenery in the American West without rushing. You'll enter the park from the north (from Yellowstone's South Entrance) and work your way south to Jackson, hitting every iconic viewpoint along the way at your own pace.

#### Attractions & Stops

**Morning (depart West Yellowstone 7:30 AM):**

- Drive south from West Yellowstone through Yellowstone's south section (~1.5 hrs)
- Enter Grand Teton National Park via **North Entrance (Moran Junction)**
- **Oxbow Bend** — first stop; stunning Teton reflection in still water, especially beautiful in morning light
- **Signal Mountain Summit Road** *(optional — 8 km paved side road, drive to top)* — panoramic 360° view of the entire Teton Range and Jackson Hole Valley from above; completely driveable to summit

**Mid-Morning:**

- **Jenny Lake** — allow 45–60 minutes here
  - Walk to lakefront for the classic Teton-over-water reflection shot (~200 m from South lot)
  - Optional: sit at the lakeside benches and enjoy the view — no rush today
  - Optional: Jenny Lake Visitor Center (free, small but worthwhile)
- **String Lake Picnic Area** — beautiful spot for a mid-morning snack break; calm lake, Teton backdrop

**Late Morning – Lunch:**

- **Schwabacher Landing** — drive the short dirt road to the river; iconic beaver pond reflection of the Tetons (~200 m flat walk from lot)
- **Lunch at Dornan's in Moose** — casual outdoor patio with one of the best Teton views from any restaurant (~$40 USD / ~$55 CAD for two)

**Afternoon:**

- **Snake River Overlook** — the Ansel Adams viewpoint; roadside pullout, ~30 m walk, unmissable
- **Mormon Row Historic District** — T.A. Moulton Barn with the Tetons rising behind it; one of the most photographed spots in the USA. Walk to the barns (~50–150 m from roadside parking)
- **Antelope Flats Road** — slow drive through open sage flats; excellent pronghorn, bison, and bald eagle sightings from the car
- **National Museum of Wildlife Art** (optional, Jackson) — Entry ~$15 USD pp; stunning building perched above the National Elk Refuge; architecture alone is worth the stop

**Late Afternoon – Evening:**

- Arrive **Jackson** (~4:00–5:00 PM)
- Walk Jackson Town Square (elk antler arches) — ~5 min from hotel
- Browse galleries and shops on Broadway/Cache St
- Enjoy a relaxed dinner

## Important Notes & Tips

### National Parks Pass

- **America the Beautiful Annual Pass:** $80 USD (~$110 CAD)
- Covers 1 vehicle and all passengers — perfect for couples!
- Valid at: Yellowstone, Grand Teton, Badlands, Wind Cave, Devils Tower, Mount Rushmore, Indiana Dunes
- Purchase at first park (Badlands on Day 5) or buy online beforehand at store.usgs.gov/recreationpass
- Physical pass ships within 3 weeks — allow time or buy at park
- Keep pass in glove box for easy access at park entrances

### EV Charging Strategy for Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV

- PHEV charging planned at: Mackinaw City, Rapid City, Cody, West Yellowstone (3 nights), Sioux Falls, Des Moines, Chicago, Kalamazoo
- All Level 2 overnight charging at hotels (220V, 3–5 hour full charge)
- Cost: Most hotel chargers are complimentary or $5–10 per session
- Can run hybrid mode continuously if needed — don't worry about charging gaps
- ChargePoint and PlugShare apps — find public charging stations en route
- Electric range: ~35–40 km per charge; use for city driving to save gas
- **Tip:** Request room near charging station when checking in

### Peak Summer Crowds (July)

- July is **peak tourist season** across all parks — expect crowds every day, not just holidays
- Yellowstone: Arrive at each site before 8:00 AM to beat parking congestion
- Grand Teton: Snake River Overlook, Jenny Lake, and Mormon Row are extremely popular — go early or late afternoon
- Badlands: Much lighter crowds than Yellowstone; mornings are still best
- **Start early every day** — the difference between a stress-free visit and a chaotic one is arriving before 9 AM

### Money-Saving Tips

1. **Hotel breakfasts:** All hotels include free breakfast — saves $20–30/day CAD
2. **Pack lunches:** Buy groceries for in-park days (bread, deli meat, cheese, fruit, snacks)
3. **Gas apps:** Use GasBuddy or Waze for cheapest gas stations (can save $10–20/tank)
4. **Annual Pass:** Saves $70+ USD vs buying individual park entries
5. **Off-peak dining:** Eat lunch as main meal (dinner prices are 20–30% higher)
6. **Free water:** Refill bottles at park visitor centres instead of buying
7. **Group discounts:** Ask about CAA/AAA discounts at hotels and attractions
8. **Credit card rewards:** Use travel rewards card for USD purchases (check foreign transaction fees)

### Border Crossing Tips

**Entering USA (Day 1):**

- Bring passports (REQUIRED — no exceptions)
- NEXUS cards if you have them (use NEXUS lanes for faster processing)
- Border wait times: Check cbp.gov/bwt before crossing
- Best crossing points: Ambassador Bridge (Detroit/Windsor) or Blue Water Bridge (Port Huron/Sarnia)
- Peak wait times: Weekdays 7–9 AM, 4–6 PM; Weekends 10 AM–4 PM
- Have hotel address ready for first night
- Declare all food items (no fresh fruits/vegetables allowed into USA)
- **July 4 tip:** Cross early (before 8 AM) — Independence Day brings heavier border traffic

**Returning to Canada (Day 15):**

- Duty-free exemption after 48+ hours: $800 CAD per person
- Declare ALL purchases (receipts ready)
- Alcohol limit: 1.5L wine OR 1.14L spirits OR 8.5L beer per person
- ArriveCAN — check current requirements before trip at cbsa-asfc.gc.ca

### Weather Considerations (Early–Mid July)

- **Yellowstone elevations:** 2,000–3,000m (6,500–9,800 ft)
- Temperature ranges:
  - Yellowstone: 5–25°C days, -2 to 5°C nights
  - South Dakota: 20–30°C days, 10–15°C nights
  - Chicago: 22–28°C, humid
- Afternoon thunderstorms common in mountains (2–4 PM) — plan to be back at car by 2 PM
- Snow possible at high elevations even in July
- Layering is key: Start day with jacket, strip to t-shirt by noon
- UV intensity is significantly stronger at high elevation (SPF 50+ essential)
- Wildfire season: Check air quality forecasts at AirNow.gov

- Oil change if due within trip dates
- Check tire pressure and tread depth (including spare)
- Top off all fluids (coolant, brake fluid, windshield washer)
- Test lights, wipers, horn; check battery health
- Verify PHEV charging cable is in vehicle

### Cell Phone Coverage — Know the Dead Zones

**Good coverage:** All major cities and highways, I-90 corridor in South Dakota, Chicago area, Michigan

**Spotty/No coverage:** Yellowstone (60% of park has no service — Verizon best), Grand Teton (limited to main roads), rural Wyoming (large gaps between towns), Badlands (visitor centres only)

**Solutions:**

- Download offline maps for entire route
- Screenshot hotel addresses/phone numbers
- Print physical itinerary
- Let family know you may be unreachable for days at a time

### Food Storage & Wildlife Safety — CRITICAL

**Wildlife Distance Rules:**

- 25 metres (75 feet): Bison, elk, deer, moose, bighorn sheep
- 100 metres (300 feet): Bears, wolves, coyotes
- Use zoom lens for photos — NOT zoom feet!

**Food Storage in Parks:**

- Never leave food in open vehicle
- Store in hard-sided containers or trunk
- Use bear-proof trash cans
- Don't eat in car then leave windows down

**If Wildlife Approaches:**

- Stay calm, don't run; back away slowly
- Get in vehicle if available
- Bear spray only as last resort (within 7–10m)

### Driving Safety — Mountain & Rural Roads

- Speed limits: Strictly enforced in national parks (45 mph / 70 km/h max)
- Many mountain roads have sharp hairpin turns (10–20 mph / 15–30 km/h)
- Use low gear on descents to avoid brake fade
- Wildlife crossings: Dawn/dusk most dangerous — scan roadsides constantly
- In Wyoming, gas stations can be 100+ km apart — fill at half tank

### Health & Safety

**Altitude Sickness:**

- Yellowstone is 2,300m (7,500 ft)
- Symptoms: Headache, nausea, fatigue, dizziness
- Prevention: Hydrate (3–4L water/day), avoid alcohol first 24h, rest frequently

**Medical Facilities in Yellowstone:**

- Clinics at Mammoth, Old Faithful, Lake (May–Sept only)
- Emergency: Dial 911 from any park phone
- Nearest hospitals: Jackson (WY), Cody (WY), Bozeman (MT)
