- Bison herds, wolves, grizzly bears, elk, pronghorn — visible from car and roadside

### Day 8 — Saturday, July 11, 2026
**Yellowstone National Park — Day 2: Upper Loop** 🌋
**In-park distance:** ~180 km | **Driving:** Full day with stops

> Start at **6:00 AM** — July peak season, parking at Norris and Mammoth fills early.

#### Route & Stops (West → North → East → South)

**🌡️ 6:15 AM — Norris Geyser Basin**
- Most thermally active basin in Yellowstone — constantly changing features
- **Porcelain Basin loop:** 0.8 km flat boardwalk; dramatic white silica landscape with steaming vents → **do this one**
- Back Basin: 1.5 km loop to Steamboat Geyser (world's tallest active geyser) → **do first 400 m to Steamboat only**, then return

**🌁 8:30 AM — Mammoth Hot Springs**
- **Lower Terraces boardwalk loop:** 0.6 km flat — most colourful active travertine terraces; parking lot → start: 50 m → **do this**
- **Upper Terraces:** Drive the one-way loop road; park at pullouts and walk 50–100 m to each terrace formation → **drive-through, stop at pullouts**
- Elk commonly graze on the lawns — keep distance, don't approach

**🏔️ 11:00 AM — Tower-Roosevelt area scenic drive**
- Drive through Roosevelt Country — open valleys, wildlife pullouts, great elk and bison viewing from car

**🌊 1:00 PM — Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone**
- **Artist Point** — 150 m flat paved path from lot to viewpoint — iconic view of Lower Falls plunging 94 m into the yellow canyon. Don't miss this
- **Lookout Point** — 200 m easy walk to canyon rim for a different perspective on the canyon depth
- ❌ Skip Uncle Tom's Trail (328 steps straight down and back up — very strenuous)
- ⚠️ Skip Brink of Lower Falls (steep and strenuous; Artist Point gives equal drama at a fraction of the effort)

**🦬 4:00 PM — Hayden Valley**
- Prime wildlife viewing: large bison herds, elk, grizzly bears (dawn/dusk best, but afternoon still productive)
- Pull off at designated wildlife pullouts — 0 m walking; view from roadside
- Allow 45–60 min to drive slowly through the valley

**6:00 PM** — Return to West Yellowstone

### Day 9 — Sunday, July 12, 2026
**Grand Teton National Park — Full Dedicated Day** 🏔️
**Route:** West Yellowstone → Grand Teton → Jackson, WY
**Distance:** ~180 km | **Drive:** ~2.5 hrs driving + full day in park

> With Lamar Valley done on Day 6, today is 100% Grand Teton — the most photogenic mountain scenery in the American West. Enter from the north, work south to Jackson. No backtracking.

#### Route & Stops (North → South, no backtracking)

**7:30 AM** — Depart West Yellowstone. Drive south through Yellowstone to South Entrance.

**🌅 9:00 AM — Oxbow Bend (first stop entering Grand Teton)**
- Roadside pullout on Hwy 89, north of Moran Junction
- ~30 m from car — Mount Moran reflected in the still Snake River
- Morning light at this hour is perfect for photography

**🏔️ 9:30 AM — Signal Mountain Summit Road**
- Drive the 8 km paved side road to the summit — completely driveable; no hiking needed
- Park at summit lot → 50 m walk to overlook rail
- Extraordinary 360° panoramic view: entire Teton Range, Jackson Hole Valley, Snake River bends

**🏊 11:00 AM — Jenny Lake**
- Park at Jenny Lake South lot → 200 m flat walk to lakefront
- Classic Teton-over-water reflection; iconic photograph
- Sit at lakeside benches — no rush today. Optional: Jenny Lake Visitor Centre (free)
- **String Lake Picnic Area** (200 m north) — calm lake, Teton backdrop; great snack break stop

**🦫 12:30 PM — Schwabacher Landing**
- Drive the short dirt access road from Hwy 89
- 200 m flat gravel walk to beaver pond reflections of the Tetons
- Some of the best landscape photography in the entire park

**🍽️ 1:30 PM — Lunch at Dornan's, Moose**
- Casual outdoor patio with an unmatched Teton view — the best restaurant view in the park
- ~$40 USD (~$55 CAD) for two

**📸 2:30 PM — Snake River Overlook**
- Roadside pullout on Hwy 89 — ~30 m from car
- The exact viewpoint Ansel Adams photographed in 1942 — Tetons reflected over the braided river

**🏚️ 3:15 PM — Mormon Row Historic District**
- T.A. Moulton Barn with the Tetons rising directly behind it — one of the most photographed barns in the world
- Roadside dirt parking → ~100–150 m flat walk to main barn; another 150 m to second barn

**🦌 4:00 PM — Antelope Flats Road**
- 5 km scenic drive through open sage flats
- Pronghorn, bison, bald eagles from car windows — 0 m walking

**🎨 Optional 5:00 PM — National Museum of Wildlife Art, Jackson**
- Entry: ~$15 USD/pp (~$41 CAD for two); stunning building above the National Elk Refuge
- 100 m from parking to entrance; all indoor

**~5:30 PM** — Arrive Jackson. Walk Town Square elk antler arches (~50 m). Browse galleries on Broadway/Cache St.

## Important Notes & Tips

### EV Charging Strategy — Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV

**Overnight Level 2 charging confirmed at:**
Mackinaw City (Day 1) · Rapid City (Days 3–4) · Cody (Day 5) · West Yellowstone (Days 6–8) · Jackson (Day 9) · Gillette (Day 10) · Sioux Falls (Day 11) · Minneapolis (Day 12) · Wisconsin Dells/Chula Vista (Days 13–14) · Chicago (Days 15–16)

- All Level 2 (220V) overnight — full charge in 3–5 hours
- Most hotel chargers complimentary or $5–10/session
- PHEV electric range: ~35–40 km per charge; use electric mode in cities and park loops to save gas
- Hybrid mode runs indefinitely on gasoline if a charging gap occurs — never a concern
- Apps: **ChargePoint** and **PlugShare** for en-route top-ups
- **In Yellowstone and Grand Teton:** No charging in-park. West Yellowstone and Jackson hotels handle this
- In Wyoming between Jackson and Gillette: fill gas tank in Jackson before departing — stations sparse

### National Parks Pass

- **America the Beautiful Annual Pass: $80 USD (~$110 CAD)**
- Buy online at store.usgs.gov/recreationpass (ships 3 weeks) or at first park entrance
- Covers: Yellowstone · Grand Teton · Badlands · Devils Tower · Wind Cave · Mount Rushmore (parking) · Indiana Dunes
- Valid for 1 vehicle + all passengers; keep in glove box
- **Saves approximately $120+ USD** vs individual entry fees

### Border Crossing Tips

**Entering USA — Day 1 (July 4):**
- Passports required — no exceptions
- NEXUS cards if you have them (dedicated NEXUS lanes; typically 5-min vs 45-min wait)
- Independence Day = busier than normal; cross by 8:30 AM or wait until after 7:00 PM
- No fresh fruits or vegetables across the border
- Hotel address for first night ready to state
- Check wait times: cbp.gov/bwt

**Returning to Canada — Day 17 (July 19/Sunday):**
- Duty-free exemption: $800 CAD/person after 48+ hours outside Canada
- Declare ALL purchases; have receipts organised
- Alcohol limit: 1.14L spirits OR 1.5L wine OR 8.5L beer per person
- Blue Water Bridge (Port Huron/Sarnia) typically shorter Sunday afternoon waits than Ambassador Bridge
- Check: cbsa-asfc.gc.ca · ArriveCAN if still required — verify before trip

### Peak Summer Crowds — July

- **Yellowstone:** Arrive before 8:00 AM at each site. Parking lots fill by 8:30 AM. Old Faithful and Grand Prismatic are worst.
- **Grand Teton:** Jenny Lake, Snake River Overlook, Mormon Row — go early (before 9 AM) or late afternoon (after 4 PM)
- **Badlands:** Lighter crowds; mornings still best for light and temperature
- **Wisconsin Dells:** Book canyon tours online at least 1 week in advance for peak July slots
- **Chicago:** Millennium Park and Navy Pier are busiest 11 AM–4 PM on summer weekends; morning visits better

### Money-Saving Tips

1. **Free hotel breakfasts** — all 16 hotels include breakfast; saves ~$25/day = ~$400 CAD total
2. **Pack lunches for park days** — buy groceries in West Yellowstone on Day 6 evening for Days 7 & 8
3. **GasBuddy app** — find cheapest gas along route; can save $10–20 per fill
4. **SpotHero app** — pre-book Chicago parking; saves $15–25/night vs drive-up
5. **CTA day pass Chicago** — $7.50 USD/pp covers unlimited rides; far cheaper than parking/Uber
6. **Off-peak dining** — lunch as main meal (dinner 20–30% more expensive)
7. **Free water** — refill at all national park visitor centres; no need to buy bottles
8. **CAA/AAA discounts** — ask at every hotel and attraction; typically 5–15% off

### Weather — July

- **West Yellowstone/Grand Teton:** Days 5–25°C; nights −2 to 8°C. Pack fleece and light rain jacket. Afternoon thunderstorms 2–4 PM — be back at car by 2 PM
- **South Dakota plains:** 25–35°C; can be very hot. Stay hydrated
- **Chicago:** 22–30°C, humid. Comfortable for walking
- **UV intensity:** 25–30% stronger at Yellowstone's 2,300 m elevation — SPF 50+ essential; reapply every 2 hours
- **Wildfire smoke:** Check AirNow.gov — can affect park visibility in dry July years

### Health & Safety — Altitude

- Yellowstone averages 2,300 m (7,500 ft) — most people feel mild effects
- Symptoms: headache, mild fatigue, slight nausea
- Prevention: drink 3–4L water/day; avoid alcohol on Day 6 arrival evening; move slowly
- Treatment: ibuprofen, hydration; effects usually pass within 24 hrs
- Medical clinics in park: Mammoth, Old Faithful, Lake (May–Sept only)

### Wildlife Safety

| Distance | Animals |
|---|---|
| 25 m minimum | Bison, elk, deer, moose, pronghorn, bighorn sheep |
| 100 m minimum | Bears, wolves, coyotes |

- Stay in vehicle unless at designated viewing areas
- Never feed wildlife — $5,000+ fine
- Bison cause more injuries than any other Yellowstone animal — they look slow; they aren't
- Wildlife jams: Pull fully off road, stay in vehicle, be patient (can last 30–60 min)
- Bear spray: Effective within 7–10 m; carry in bear country even if not hiking
