### Day 7 — Friday, July 10, 2026
**Cody, WY → Beartooth Highway → Lamar Valley → West Yellowstone, MT** 🏔️
**Distance:** ~343 km | **Drive:** ~5h 10min
*(Source: Your verified Google Maps screenshot — same route confirmed)*

> **This is the most spectacular driving day of the entire trip.** The route combines two of the greatest scenic drives in North America: the **Chief Joseph Scenic Byway** (WY-296, 74 km) and the **Beartooth Highway** (US-212, 111 km Red Lodge to Cooke City), then enters Yellowstone via the Northeast Entrance through **Lamar Valley**, and exits via the park interior to West Yellowstone.
>
> **Beartooth Highway:** Called "the most beautiful drive in America" by CBS News correspondent Charles Kuralt. Crests at **Beartooth Pass, 3,337 m elevation** (10,947 ft) — highest paved road in Wyoming. Snow possible even in July. Check road status at nps.gov/yell before departing.
>
> **No museums today** — the scenery IS the attraction. Skip Buffalo Bill Center entirely. Full day for driving and wildlife.
>
> ⛽ Zero gas stations between Cody and West Yellowstone. Fill tank completely in Cody the night before.

#### Route & Stops — Detailed

**8:00 AM — Depart Cody** (full tank from night before)

**8:20 AM — Turn onto WY-120 North** (17 km north of Cody)

**8:35 AM — Enter Chief Joseph Scenic Byway (WY-296)**
- 74 km through Shoshone National Forest; dramatic canyon and high desert mountain scenery
- **Dead Indian Summit overlook** (~10 km in): panoramic view of Absaroka Mountains and Sunlight Basin; roadside pullout ~30 m from car
- **Sunlight Bridge**: highest bridge in Wyoming (over Sunlight Creek Gorge, 120 m deep); pullout ~50 m from car — must stop
- **Cathedral Cliffs**: stunning limestone spire formations; roadside viewpoint ~30 m

**~10:30 AM — Junction with US-212 (Beartooth Highway)**
- Turn RIGHT (east) onto US-212 toward Red Lodge — this is the Beartooth Highway
- Drive east ~18 km to **Beartooth Pass (3,337 m)** — switchbacks, glaciers, alpine lakes, snowfields even in July
- **Beartooth Pass overlook**: park at summit area; walk 100–200 m to viewpoints. Extraordinary alpine tundra landscape
- **Top of the World Store** (near summit): last stop before descent; restrooms, snacks
- Take photos, absorb the view, then turn around and head back west to US-212 Junction

**~12:30 PM — Continue west on US-212 toward Cooke City, MT**
- Pass through Cooke City and Silvergate — small gateway towns; lunch option if desired (~$20 USD for two)
- Enter Yellowstone National Park via **Northeast Entrance** — pass covered by America the Beautiful Pass

**~1:30 PM — 🦬 Lamar Valley**
- Drive slowly through the full 30 km valley — "Serengeti of North America"
- Bison herds, wolves (most reliably spotted here), grizzly bears, elk, pronghorn — all from car and roadside pullouts
- Stop at pullouts wherever herds appear (0–30 m from car)
- Early afternoon light still good for photography

**~3:30 PM — Continue south through park**
- Tower Junction → Norris (brief drive-past) → Madison → West Entrance
- Optional: stop at **Gibbon Falls** viewpoint on Gibbon River (roadside pullout, 30 m from car)

**~5:30 PM** — Exit West Entrance; arrive West Yellowstone

## Important Notes & Tips

### EV Charging — Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV

**Overnight Level 2 charging confirmed at:**
Mackinaw City (Day 1) · Rapid City (Days 4–5) · Cody (Day 6) · West Yellowstone (Days 7–9) · Jackson (Day 10) · Gillette (Day 11) · Sioux Falls (Days 3 + 12) · Minneapolis (Day 13) · Wisconsin Dells/Chula Vista (Days 14–15) · Chicago (Days 16–17)

- All Level 2 (220V) overnight — full charge in 3–5 hours
- Most hotel chargers: complimentary or $5–10/session
- PHEV electric range: ~35–40 km per charge; use electric mode in cities
- Hybrid mode runs indefinitely on gasoline — never a concern
- Apps: **ChargePoint** and **PlugShare** for en-route top-ups
- ⚠️ Wyoming between Jackson and Gillette: fill tank in Jackson — stations sparse
- ⚠️ Cody to West Yellowstone: zero stations on Beartooth route — fill night before

### National Parks Pass

- **$80 USD** at store.usgs.gov/recreationpass (ships 3 weeks) or first park entrance
- Covers: Yellowstone · Grand Teton · Badlands · Devils Tower · Wind Cave · Mount Rushmore (parking) · Indiana Dunes
- Saves **~$100+ USD** vs individual fees
- Keep in glove box — present at each park entrance gate

### Beartooth Highway — Day 7 Specific Tips

- Check road status before departing: nps.gov/yell/planyourvisit/parkroads.htm or call 307-344-2117
- Pass (3,337 m elevation) can have snow any month — dress in layers
- Drive slowly on switchbacks: 30–50 km/h; single-lane sections exist
- The round-trip detour to Beartooth Pass summit from the Chief Joseph junction adds ~1.5–2 hours but is the scenic highlight of the entire highway
- Fill gas in Cody the night before — zero stations on the entire Beartooth/Chief Joseph route

### Border Crossing Tips

**Entering USA — Day 1 (July 4):**
- Passports required (no exceptions)
- NEXUS cards: dedicated NEXUS lanes; typically 5 min vs 45+ min wait
- Independence Day = busier than normal; cross before 8:30 AM or after 7:00 PM
- No fresh fruits or vegetables into USA
- Have first hotel address ready
- Check wait times: cbp.gov/bwt

**Returning Canada — Day 18:**
- Duty-free: $800 CAD/person after 48+ hours outside Canada
- Declare ALL purchases; receipts organized
- Alcohol: 1.14L spirits OR 1.5L wine OR 8.5L beer per person
- Blue Water Bridge (Port Huron/Sarnia) typically shorter weekday waits
- Check: cbsa-asfc.gc.ca · Verify ArriveCAN requirements before trip

### Peak Summer Crowds — July

- **Yellowstone:** Arrive before 8:00 AM at each site. Grand Prismatic and Old Faithful parking fill by 8:30 AM
- **Grand Teton:** Jenny Lake, Snake River Overlook, Mormon Row — before 9 AM or after 4 PM
- **Badlands:** Lighter crowds; mornings still best for light
- **Wisconsin Dells:** Book canyon tours 1+ week in advance for July
- **Chicago:** Millennium Park / Navy Pier busiest 11 AM–4 PM on summer weekends

### Money-Saving Tips

1. **Free hotel breakfasts** — all 17 hotels; saves ~$25 USD/day = ~$425 USD total
2. **Pack lunches for park days** (Days 8–10) — buy groceries in West Yellowstone Day 7 evening
3. **GasBuddy app** — find cheapest gas; can save $10–20 per fill
4. **SpotHero app** — pre-book Chicago parking; saves $15–25/night
5. **CTA day pass Chicago** — $7.50 USD/pp unlimited rides; far cheaper than parking
6. **Off-peak dining** — lunch as main meal; dinner 20–30% more expensive

### Wildlife Safety

| Distance | Animals |
|---|---|
| 25 m minimum | Bison, elk, deer, moose, pronghorn, bighorn sheep |
| 100 m minimum | Bears, wolves, coyotes |

- Bison cause more injuries than any other Yellowstone animal — never approach
- Wildlife jams: pull fully off road; stay in vehicle; can last 30–60 min
- Bear spray: carry when outside vehicle in bear country

### Weather — July

- **Yellowstone (2,300 m):** Days 5–25°C; nights −2 to 8°C; afternoon thunderstorms 2–4 PM; be back at car by 2 PM
- **Beartooth Pass (3,337 m):** Can snow any month; pack warm layers for Day 7 summit visit
- **South Dakota plains:** 25–35°C; very hot; stay hydrated
- **Chicago:** 22–30°C, humid; comfortable for walking
- **UV:** 25–30% stronger at Yellowstone/Beartooth elevation; SPF 50+ essential
