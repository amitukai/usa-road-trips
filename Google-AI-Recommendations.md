Driving from [Cody, WY](https://www.codyyellowstone.org/blog/roadtrips-in-cody-yellowstone-the-beartooth-pass-and-more/) to [West Yellowstone, MT](https://roadtrippers.com/destinations/beartooth-highway/) via the [Beartooth Highway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beartooth_Highway) (US-212) is a spectacular, full-day alpine road trip. Plan for 8 to 10 hours of total travel time, encompassing 195 miles of driving, frequent scenic stops, and potential wildlife/traffic delays inside the park. [1, 2, 3, 4] 
## 1. The Route Breakdown

* Leg 1: Cody to Beartooth Pass (via Chief Joseph Scenic Byway)
* Distance/Time: ~70 miles | 1.5 to 2 hours
   * Details: Head north out of Cody on WY-120, then take WY-296 (the Chief Joseph Scenic Byway). This highly rated scenic road takes you across the Sunlight Bridge and up to the junction of US-212. [5, 6, 7, 8, 9] 
* Leg 2: The Beartooth Highway
* Distance/Time: ~68 miles | 2 to 3 hours
   * Details: Turn east/north on US-212 to climb the highway, switchbacking up to the Beartooth Pass (elevation 10,947 ft). The drive levels off at the alpine plateau before descending to the town of Cooke City, MT (the Northeast Entrance of Yellowstone). [4, 10, 11, 12] 
* Leg 3: Through Yellowstone to West Yellowstone
* Distance/Time: ~60 miles | 2.5 to 3+ hours
   * Details: Enter Yellowstone and drive the famed Lamar Valley (excellent for wildlife), continuing west through Mammoth Hot Springs, Norris, and Madison until you reach the West Entrance. [1, 13, 14, 15, 16] 

## 2. Crucial Travel Tips

* Seasonality: The Beartooth Highway features heavy, lingering snowfall and is generally only open for automobiles from late May (Memorial Day weekend) through mid-October, weather permitting. Always check for road closures or active weather alerts before departing. [4, 5, 17] 
* Elevation & Weather: Temperatures plunge at 11,000 feet. You might experience sunshine in Cody, but freezing winds or snow on the pass itself. [12, 17, 18, 19] 
* Yellowstone Passes: You will need a standard Yellowstone National Park pass to complete the transit through the park. [10]

* Chief Joseph Scenic Byway (WY-296): As you climb out of Cody, stop briefly at the [Sunlight Creek Bridge](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/g/1hc1q7m53). At 300 feet above the canyon floor, it is the highest infrastructure bridge in Wyoming.

## 🗺️ Outbound Optimizations (Days 1–5)## Day 1 & Day 2: The Michigan Transit Loop

* 
* The Issue: Your route takes you across the busy [Ambassador](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/01fnrv) or [Blue Water bridge](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/02hbnd) on July 4th (U.S. Independence Day), then sends you all the way up I-75 to [Mackinaw City](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/013dzr), only to cross the [Mackinac Bridge](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/g/11nxrzbtk8) the next morning to go west through the Upper Peninsula (UP).
* The Fix: Stick to the plan but cross at the [Blue Water Bridge](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/02hbnd) (Sarnia/[Port Huron](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0vqcq)). It traditionally handles holiday cottage traffic better than the commercial truck lanes of the [Ambassador Bridge](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/01fnrv) in Detroit.
* PHEV Tip: Set your Outlander to "Save" or "Charge" mode on the long I-75 stretch. Save your pure EV battery for the slow, stop-and-go holiday traffic you will inevitably encounter around Mackinaw City's waterfront and [Mackinac Island](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0vc38) ferry docks.

## Day 3: The I-90 Flatline ([Wisconsin Dells](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0pk05) to [Rapid City](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0_rwf))

* 
* The Optimization: The [Mitchell Corn Palace](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/04cmb2) is a fun, quirky 15-minute leg stretcher. However, right before you reach [Rapid City](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0_rwf), you will drive directly past the town of [Wall, SD](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0_rx6). Do not just look at the bumper stickers—pull off for 20 minutes at the legendary [Wall Drug Store](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0351zq). It is a massive, historic cowboy-themed drug store complex that serves as the ultimate American roadside subculture experience.

## Day 4: The Black Hills Loop

* 
* The Rationale: Your itinerary drives [Mount Rushmore](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/019dvv) first, then [Custer State Park](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/08_nwy), and [Crazy Horse](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0373w4) third. This is geographically perfect, but order is everything.
* The Fix: Arrive at [Mount Rushmore](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/019dvv) exactly when the parking structures open (around 7:30 AM). Morning sun hits the faces perfectly for photography. When driving Custer's [Wildlife Loop Road](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/g/1vnrr2xj), keep your PHEV in EV Mode. The near-silent electric powertrain allows you to glide past bison, pronghorns, and the famous "begging burros" without the noise of an internal combustion engine disrupting the wildlife.
* Route Highlight: Ensure your return route to [Rapid City](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0_rwf) via Iron Mountain Road (US-16A) is done from south to north. The historic tunnels on this road were structurally engineered to perfectly frame [Mount Rushmore](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/019dvv) in the distance as you drive through them.
* 

## Day 5: Badlands Backtracking Fix

* 
* The Issue: The itinerary has you driving 1 hour east from [Rapid City](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0_rwf) to [Badlands National Park](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/019fdr) in the morning, then driving 2.5 hours west past [Rapid City](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0_rwf) to get to [Devils Tower](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/g/11g1b1hxy8). This creates a 100 km backtracking loop.
* The Fix: Flip your schedule. Wake up at 5:30 AM and drive straight to the [Badlands](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/019fdr) for sunrise. The morning light makes the yellow and pink mounds pop beautifully, and you bypass the scorching midday [Badlands](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/019fdr) heat. By 9:30 AM, hit I-90 West, bypass [Rapid City](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0_rwf) entirely, and cruise straight toward [Devils Tower National Monument](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/g/11g1b1hxy8).
*

## 🌋 Yellowstone & Grand Teton Efficiency (Days 7–9)## Day 7: Lower Loop Strategy

* 
* The Bottleneck: [Grand Prismatic](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/064gsx) ([Midway Geyser Basin](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/g/1q5bnd98h)) and [Old Faithful](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0164xv) are absolute gridlocks by 9:00 AM in July.
* The Fix: You must enter the West Entrance gate by 6:30 AM at the latest. Drive straight past [Fountain Paint Pot](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/06230z) and park at [Grand Prismatic](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/064gsx) boardwalk first.
* The Caveat: Early mornings at [Grand Prismatic](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/064gsx) can be very foggy due to steam hitting the cold mountain air. If it is too misty to see the colors, pivot immediately to [Old Faithful](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/0164xv), complete the [Upper Geyser Basin](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/g/121v8kkm) boardwalk, and return to [Grand Prismatic](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/064gsx) around 11:30 AM when the overhead summer sun burns off the steam and illuminates the bacterial mats.

## Day 8: Upper Loop & Grand Canyon

* 
* Route Highlight: Your itinerary is spot on—[Norris Geyser Basin](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/g/11h95hfz84) is the hottest, most volatile basin in the park. Hit it early.
* The Optimization: When visiting the Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone, your document highlights [Artist Point](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/m/010pqd67) (South Rim). To perfectly balance this, make a quick drive-up stop at [Brink of the Upper Falls](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/g/11bv3yjfnv) on the North Rim. Unlike the strenuous Lower Falls trail, the [Upper Falls](https://www.google.com/search?kgmid=/g/11cmyb2dvz) path is short, mostly flat, and lets you stand inches away from the massive river right before it drops over the precipice.
